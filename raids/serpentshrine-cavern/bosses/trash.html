A. Underbog Colossus
Kill Priority
  1. Underbog Colossus.
  2. Death-spawned adds / Ragers.
Own Colossus calls
Colossus appears throughout the raid, so it gets its own section. Call the ability pair or death effect, then react fast.
Death adds
On death, Colossus can spawn many Ragers or other adds. Off tank gathers them and the raid cleaves quickly.
Cleanse and cover
Initial Infection is a disease DoT; cleanse it quickly. Atrophic Blow reduces Strength and Stamina, and Frenzy increases attack speed.
Parasite handling
Serpentshrine Parasite spawns a parasite after a delay. Call it, control it, and kill it without losing the Colossus tank.
Move for raid damage
Acid Geyser targets a random player; that player runs to the tank / Colossus position to avoid spraying the raid. Spore Quake has very large range, so move out fast.
Avoid poison
Toxic Pool is ground poison; move out. Swap quickly to Death-spawned adds / Ragers when the Colossus dies.
B. Pre-Hydross Trash
Kill Priority
  1. Hate-Screamers + Sporebats, cleaved together.
  2. Beast-Tamer.
Beast-Tamer pack
Main tank normally picks up Coilfang Beast-Tamer and faces it away because it cleaves. Off tank(s) pick up the other mobs for cleave.
Hold cleave targets
Sporebats and Hate-Screamers get cleaved together. Keep them stacked and controlled while the Beast-Tamer is faced away from the raid.
Silence and charge damage
Coilfang Hate-Screamer uses Sonic Scream and a 5-second AoE Silence. Serpentshrine Sporebats charge the furthest target, stun, and apply Spore Burst Nature splash plus DoT.
Cleave first pack targets
Kill Hate-Screamers and Sporebats together. Stay in range for control, avoid being the furthest target when possible, and do not split damage away from the cleave stack.
Bestial Wrath
Beast-Tamer uses Bestial Wrath, increasing its damage and empowering the two Sporebats. Burn it after the cleave targets are under control.
C. Pre-Lurker Trash
Kill Priority
  1. Shatterer.
  2. Honor Guard.
  3. Sheeped Priestesses.
  4. Technician.
Honor Guard positioning
Vashj’ir Honor Guard has Enrage / Frenzy, melee-range Frightening Shout, Knockback, and Mortal Cleave. Face Honor Guards away and use wall or pillar positioning when possible.
Stacked platform packs
These platform packs above Lurker are usually stacked and AoE’d, but tanks need time to establish threat before DPS opens. Greyheart Technician applies Hamstring.
Priestess and Spirit
Coilfang Priestess casts Holy Smite and Holy Fire; dispel Holy Fire. On death she spawns a Spirit that looks like Spirit of Redemption but does not mean a player priest died, and it spams Holy Nova to damage the raid and heal enemies.
Fear support
Tremor Totem and Fear Ward help stabilize Honor Guard fear. Keep tanks topped before Knockback and Mortal Cleave overlap.
Control Shatter Armor
Coilfang Shatterer applies Shatter Armor, reducing tank armor by 50% for 15 seconds. Grounding Totem can prevent Shatter Armor, then kill Shatterer before Sheeped Priestesses.
AoE after control
Wait for tank threat and priority targets before broad AoE. Cleave only after Shatterer pressure and Sheeped Priestesses are handled.
D. Pre-Leotheras Trash
Kill Priority
  1. Tidecaller.
  2. Water Elemental Totem immediately when it appears.
  3. Skulker.
  4. Fathom-Witch.
  5. Sheeped Nether-Mage.
  6. Shield-Bearer.
  7. Serpentguard.
  8. Banished Lurker last.
Control Nether-Mage
Nether-Mages should be sheeped and left controlled while the raid handles Tidecaller, Water Elemental Totem, Skulker, and Fathom-Witch first. If sheep breaks, re-control quickly.
Shield-Bearer stack
Shield-Bearer uses Shield Charge and Avenger’s Shield. Raid should stack behind Shield-Bearers to prevent charge behavior while tanks keep packs stable.
Fathom-Witch positioning
Fathom-Witch uses Domination, Shadow Bolt, and Shadow Nova knockback. Position carefully because Shadow Nova can throw players into dangerous water and fish areas.
Purge and dispel Tidecaller
Tidecaller Poison Shield should be purged or removed with Arcane Shot. Virulent Poison can be dispelled, and Water Elemental Totem must die quickly while the elemental itself is ignored.
Control Domination
Domination can be prevented or absorbed with Grounding Totem. Controlled players should be controlled quickly until the effect ends.
Kill totem, not elemental
Tidecaller drops Water Elemental Totem; kill the totem immediately when it appears and ignore the elemental itself. Purge, dispel, and swap back to the priority target.
Banish Lurkers last
Lurkers should be banished and saved for the end; do not fight them with other mobs active. Move away from mushrooms and do not waste time killing mushrooms.
Skulker, Fathom-Witch, then Serpentguard
After Tidecaller and its totem are handled, kill Skulkers before Fathom-Witches, then Serpentguards. Serpentguard uses Spell Reflection and Corrupt Devotion Aura; Nether-Mage destruction buffs can be dispelled or spellstolen, and Skulker kicks nearby casters.
E. Pre-Morogrim Trash
Kill Priority
  1. Depth-Seer.
  2. Hydromancer.
  3. Shaman.
  4. Warrior.
  5. Lurker / Harpooner / minor murlocs.
Depth-Seer first
Depth-Seer casts Healing Touch, which must be interrupted, and Rejuvenation on other murlocs. Keep it controlled until it dies.
Warrior control
Warrior Frenzy increases attack speed heavily and should be removed with Tranquilizing Shot. Bloodthirst deals damage and heals the mob, and Uppercut knocks back.
Hydromancer casts
Hydromancer uses Frost Nova, Frost Shock, and Frostbolt. Frostbolt can be interrupted to reduce tank and raid pressure.
Shaman spacing
Shaman Chain Lightning punishes stacked players, Lightning Bolt can be interrupted, and Lightning Shield damages attackers, especially auto attackers.
Interrupt healers and bolts
Assign kicks to Healing Touch first, then Frostbolt and Lightning Bolt. Kill priority casters before padding on clumped murlocs.
AoE after control
Lurker and Harpooner are mostly melee damage mobs with net / bleed style minor pressure. Minor murlocs are usually clumped with dangerous mobs and AoE’d once priority mobs are controlled.
