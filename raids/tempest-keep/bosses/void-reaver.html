Phase 1 · Pull / tank threat setup
Compete for top threat
Main tank sets Void Reaver in position while both off tanks build hard threat immediately. Knock Away can reduce the active tank's threat, so every tank must stay near the top and be ready for the boss to swap targets cleanly.
Heal all threat tanks
Keep assignments on the main tank and off tank targets from the pull. Expect tank damage to shift after Knock Away and do not tunnel one player while another tank climbs into the active threat position.
Let misdirects land
Hunters use assigned misdirects to stabilize opening threat. DPS should hold burst until tanks are established so Void Reaver does not turn into the raid after an early Knock Away.
Phase 2 · Arcane Orb / raid spread
Hold the boss steady
Keep Void Reaver stable for melee while ranged and healers spread for Arcane Orb. Only adjust facing or position if threat movement forces it; avoid dragging the boss through the raid.
Spread and move from impact
Healers stay spread with ranged, watch incoming Arcane Orb impact zones, and move away before the explosion lands. Do not stack tightly while reaching your tank or raid-healing assignments.
Ranged spread, melee stay in
Ranged DPS maintain spacing and move out of Arcane Orb impact zones without crossing through other groups. Melee stay on the boss unless orb movement, boss facing, or threat requires a controlled adjustment.
Phase 3 · Pounding / raid healing
Survive transitions
Maintain mitigation and threat through boss swings, Knock Away threat drops, and Pounding windows. If you become the active tank, settle the boss quickly so melee and healers can recover.
Pre-load raid healing
Pounding deals heavy raid-wide AoE damage near Void Reaver. Prepare raid heals before it starts, keep tanks covered during the damage, and recover melee without abandoning spread players hit by Arcane Orb.
Do not panic during Pounding
Melee use personals, healthstones, and steady positioning to survive Pounding instead of scattering through the raid. Ranged keep casting from spread positions while avoiding any incoming orb zones.
Phase 4 · Enrage / clean finish
Keep threat stable
The finish is a clean execution check: keep competing for threat, call dangerous swaps, and prevent Void Reaver from turning into the raid as Knock Away continues to pressure the tank table.
Spend mana efficiently
Use cooldowns and mana tools before the raid falls behind. Prioritize tanks, Pounding recovery, and Arcane Orb mistakes so healing errors do not snowball near the kill.
Burn without stealing threat
Void Reaver is simple if the raid avoids orb deaths and respects tank threat. Use cooldowns to finish the boss before healing and threat mistakes stack up, but watch threat during every burn window.

Void Reaver wipe causes
  • Tanks lose threat after Knock Away and Void Reaver turns into the raid.
  • Ranged stack too tightly and Arcane Orb hits multiple players.
  • Players do not move from Arcane Orb impact zones.
  • Healers fall behind during Pounding.
  • DPS pulls threat early before tanks stabilize.
  • Raid burns mana inefficiently and runs out before the kill.
